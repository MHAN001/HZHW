Family Reunion


    Just like the Christmas, Chinese Spring Festival is the time for Family Reunion. The reunion of relatives during the Spring Festival is a tradition passed down from generation to generation in China. One of the connotations expressed during the Spring Festival in Chinese traditional culture is family reunion.

Spring Festival Travel Rush


    It's the largest annual human migration in the world. Each year, between late January and early February, hundreds of millions of Chinese people head home for the country's biggest holiday, the Lunar New Year. Also called the Spring Festival, it's the one-time each year when families scattered across the country reunite, and to get to one another, China sees a travel boom on a massive scale.

Make A Ceremonial Call On New Year


    The New Year is a traditional folk custom in China. It is a way for people to welcome the new and express their best wishes. In ancient times, the original meaning of the word “New Year” was to celebrate the New Year for the elderly, including giving greetings to the elders, congratulating the New Year on wishful considerations, and greeting life well. In case of relatives and friends of the same generation, we must also give congratulations. With the development of the times, the customs of the New Year continue to add new content and forms.
    The time of New Year's greetings is generally from the first to the fifth. After passing the eighth lunar month, visits to friends and relatives are considered to be worshipping in the early years. After the fifth day of the first lunar month and the fifteenth day, the visiting relatives and friends visit the old age.

Dragon-Lion Dance


    Dragon dance is also called dragon lantern dancing and dragon dance. The dragon is an ancient totem. It is said that dragons can make clouds, rain, and disasters, symbolizing auspiciousness. Therefore, praying for peace and harvest in a dragon dance manner has become a custom throughout the country. From the Spring Festival to the Lantern Festival, there are dragon dance customs in many places. The dragon represents auspiciousness, dignity and bravery in the Chinese nation. It is also a symbol of power.

The Red Envelope


    The red envelope in the traditional sense is also called the lucky money. It is the money that the elders gave to children in red paper during the Lunar New Year. It is said that in the Ming and Qing Dynasties, most of the lucky money was given to children with a string of red rope. After the Republic of China, it evolved to be wrapped in red paper. Now it refers to the red paper package that holds money; it is used to present gift money when it is festive.
    During the Spring Festival, the red envelopes that the elders sent to juniors after 0:00 pm on the 30th of the Lunar New Year are called lucky money, which means they are bringing the blessings and luck of the new year to them. There are customary red envelopes during the Spring Festival each year. In some areas, married people send unmarried relatives and friends. The habit of issuing red envelopes generally exists only among relatively close relatives and friends.

New Year's Dinner


    New Year's Eve dinner specifically refers to the Lunar New Year's Eve dinner. It is also the most important dinner at the end of the year. The annual reunion dinner fully demonstrates the mutual respect and love of the Chinese family members. This kind of mutual respect and mutual love makes the relationship between family members even closer. The reunion of family members often makes the “homeowner” feel comforted and satisfied. The old people look at their children and grandchildren. The families are all talking about Tianlun. The care and support of their children in the past has not been in vain. What a happiness it is. Younger generations are also taking this opportunity to express their gratitude to parents for their parenting. On New Year’s Eve night, no matter how far apart, how busy the work is, people always hope to return to their homes and have a round dinner. This dinner is also called "Family Love," and it is a family banquet that people attach great importance to.
